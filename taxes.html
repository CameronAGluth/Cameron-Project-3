The Three Types of Bracket-Related Taxes:

  • Progressive
  • Flat
  • Regressive

Progressive Tax

A form of taxation where individuals with higher income pay a higher percentage of taxes. Conversely, individuals with lower income pay a lower percentage of taxes. For example, the U.S. Federal Income Tax.

Flat Tax

Everyone, regardless of their bracket, pays the same percentage. For example, the Illinois State Income Tax.

Regressive Tax

A form of taxation where individuals with higher income pay a lower percentage of taxes. Conversely, individuals with lower income pay a higher percentage of taxes. For example, sales taxes.
